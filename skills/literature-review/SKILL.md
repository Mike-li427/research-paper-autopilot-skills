# Literature Review

This skill coordinates literature review work. It is a thin, practical entry point that should use stronger installed research skills when needed: `nature-academic-search` for source discovery and citation verification, `deep-research` or `academic-research-suite` for systematic synthesis, and `survey` for reading-corpus workflows.

## Workflow

1. Define the review purpose: background paragraph, related work, grant section, survey, systematic review, or manuscript introduction.
2. Clarify scope:
   - field and subfield
   - date range
   - paper types to include
   - databases or venues
   - required citation style
3. Build search queries and inclusion/exclusion criteria.
4. Search and verify sources before making claims when current literature matters.
5. Cluster papers by method, mechanism, dataset, finding, or controversy.
6. Synthesize the literature into claims with citations, not a paper-by-paper list.
7. Identify gaps, limitations, unresolved disagreements, and the user's research opportunity.

## Output Shape

Use the smallest output that fits the task:

- Quick map: themes, key papers, gaps, next searches.
- Full review: structured sections with citation-backed claims.
- Related work: concise argument that positions the user's work.
- Evidence table: claim, supporting papers, strength, caveats.

## Guardrails

- Browse or use literature tools for current or citation-sensitive claims.
- Do not cite papers that have not been verified.
- Distinguish source-backed findings from the model's synthesis.
- Preserve the user's target journal or field conventions.
